To us, who think we are so modern and gift mimosas. 1981: the law that abrogated fascist machismo and killed Donatella Colasanti

I was only one and a half years old in September 1981. I was too young to understand the historical significance of what was happening. My perception of women, or rather of a woman, was represented by my mother's love and her breast from which, probably, I was already detaching at the time.

At school, teachers never mentioned this topic, and when as an adult I read so shamefully about the permission of the Italian law of honour killings and shotgun wedding, I was thrilled to find out that Article 587 of the Fascist Penal Code (approved in October 1930 on proposal of the Minister of Justice, Alfredo Rocco) was repealed only on September 5, 1981. Just 38 years ago!

Le Masciare - Witches from Southern Italy

The women who refused the existence of God and who were trusted because they were able to cure with healing herbs were called witches. They were women without supernatural gifts, but with vast knowledge and wisdom. For millennia, anyone who was ill was looking for a witch's remedies. Their demonisation in the Middle Ages then led, over the centuries, to various and obscure legends, including the Italian ones of the Gatta Masciare, the "envious cat".

In the alleys of the Old City of Bari and the province, the Gatta Masciare was among the most famous witches. The term that would derive from Megara, one of the three great Erinyes that is female personifications of revenge especially towards those who strike their own family and relatives, would also come f rom the Greek verb Μεγαιρα, "envy".
